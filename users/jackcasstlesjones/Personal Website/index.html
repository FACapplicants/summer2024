Hi, I'm Jack! A web developer, climber and traveler who is looking to take my next step in the wonderful world of coding

My Coding Story

In 2021, I made the crazy choice of moving to Thailand with my partner mid-COVID to teach English in public schools in Bangkok. In my teaching schedule, there were several hours of every day where I had to be in school but had no marking or teaching to do. Having messed around with FreeCodeCamp before, I got stuck into some Udemy tutorials and the rest is history!

Why Coding?

All my life I have experienced excess amounts of energy, fixations with certain topics, activities or foods, and an unwavering interest in the world around me. In 2022 my girlfriend suggested that I go forward with an ADHD diagnosis. Due to the extreme burden on the NHS, this is something I am still on a waiting list for. The prospect that I am neurodivergent has impacted me massively. From the self-forgiveness of "weird" behaviour or fixations, to being able to understand why I can become so stressed with an over-abundance of tasks (or lack of them!!), it has had a significant impact on my understanding of my own mental health.

Climbing & Coding

I love my brain. I love that I can focus intensely on one topic or long term goal. I genuinely see it as a superpower. However, there are many times when I am overloaded and I struggle to cope. As a result, I tend to seek out activities that encourage deep focus and a state of flow. One of these is rock climbing. When I am moving on rock, the volume of my thoughts is turned down to zero and I am living purely in the moment. The same happens when I am struggling to find a particular solution to a problem in coding, and the problem solving aspect of both rock climbing and coding has brought me so much joy since I began both of them three years ago.
